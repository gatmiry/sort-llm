\subsection{Attn2 Scores Are Predominantly Determined by MLP1 Output}
\label{sec:attn2-mlp1-dependence}

We observe that in the majority of leap-former checkpoints, the second-layer
attention scores are predominantly determined by the first-layer MLP output
alone. We verify this across a grid of $k \in \{16, 32\}$ and
$N \in \{128, 256, 512, 1024\}$ with 5~random seeds each (40~checkpoints
total), of which 15 are classified as leap-formers (i.e., full-sequence
accuracy drops to near zero when attn2 is ablated). We exclude two outlier
checkpoints at $N{=}1024$ discussed below, leaving 13 for detailed analysis.
For each checkpoint we evaluate on 5{,}000 randomly sampled sequences,
measuring over all output positions. We perform two complementary measurements.

\paragraph{Distributional similarity.}
We compare the attn2 probability distribution computed from the full residual
stream ($\mathbf{e} + \mathbf{a}_1 + \mathbf{m}_1$) against the distribution
computed from only the MLP1 output ($\mathbf{m}_1$), with embeddings and attn1
output zeroed out. We report the per-position $\ell_1$ distance
$d = \sum_j |p_{\text{normal}}(j) - p_{\text{mlp1-only}}(j)|$, which ranges
from~$0$ (identical) to~$2$ (disjoint). All 13 checkpoints exhibit mean
$\ell_1$ distances below $0.03$, with all five \texttt{k32\_N512} seeds
yielding $d \approx 0.005$ (Figure~\ref{fig:probl1distance}).

\paragraph{Full-model accuracy.}
As a functional test, we also measure the model's full-sequence sorting
accuracy when attn2's input is restricted to MLP1 output only (while all other
components, including MLP2 and the final readout, receive the normal residual
stream augmented with this modified attn2 output).
Figure~\ref{fig:attn2-acc-mlp1only} shows that the accuracy drop is zero or
negligible across all 13 checkpoints (maximum drop $0.05\%$), confirming that
the embedding and attn1 components of the residual stream carry no information
that attn2 functionally relies on.

\paragraph{Outliers.}
Two of the 15 leap-former checkpoints at $N = 1024$
(\texttt{k16\_N1024\_s3}, $d = 0.133$; \texttt{k32\_N1024\_s5}, $d = 0.299$)
deviate from this pattern, showing that attn2 can also learn to exploit other
residual stream components. Since other seeds at $N = 1024$ do not exhibit this
behavior, it appears to be seed-dependent rather than determined by vocabulary
size. We note that the MLP1-only dependence is a common empirical feature of
leap-formers in our setting, not a necessary architectural property. The
mechanistic analysis that follows focuses on checkpoints exhibiting this common
pattern.
